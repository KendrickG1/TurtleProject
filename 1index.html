What We Do

The Three Pillars of Conservation

Scientific Research

We study habitat needs, population dynamics, and threats to gain crucial data for conservation strategies.

Habitat Restoration

Working to restore vital river, stream, and swamp habitats damaged by pollution or human development.

Community Education

Raising awareness through local school programs and public events to foster responsible stewardship.

The Focus of Our Work

Alabama's Endangered Turtles

Red-bellied Cooter

Endangered

Alabama's state reptile, found only in the Mobile-Tensaw River Delta.

Flattened Musk Turtle

Threatened

Endemic to the Black Warrior River system, sensitive to water quality.

Chicken Turtle

Vulnerable

Relies on temporary ponds and wetlands; threatened by development.

Barbour's Map Turtle

Threatened

Inhabits large rivers in the southeast corner of Alabama and adjacent states.

Smooth Softshell

Special Concern

Known for its flat, leather-like shell; sensitive to river sedimentation.
